# How Traders Behave in Different Bitcoin Market Moods

### What I Wanted to Find Out

I was curious to know: do traders make decisions when the Bitcoin market is scared or excited?

To figure this out I combined two things:

1. The Bitcoin Fear and Greed Index, which shows how people feel about the market
2. Data from Hyperliquid traders which shows what trades they made

Then I looked at how traders profits, losses and decisions changed when the market mood was different.

---

# The Data I Used

### The Bitcoin Fear and Greed Index

This index shows how investors feel about the market.

| Score | Market Mood |
|--------|-------------|
| 0-24 | Really Scared |
| 25-44 | Scared |
| 45-55 | Neutral |
| 56-75 | Greedy |
| 76-100 | Really Greedy |

Think of it like a meter that measures the markets emotions.

### Hyperliquid Trader Data

This data includes:

- The traders account
- The coin they traded
- Whether they bought or sold
- The size of the trade
- How much they borrowed
- Whether they made a profit or loss
- The time and date of the trade

I looked at over 42,000 trades from 80 traders.

---

# What I Did

### Step 1: Clean Up the Data

I fixed the dates removed any formatting issues. Made sure the numbers were in the right format.

### Step 2: Combine the Data

For each trade I added the market mood for that day.

For example:

| Date | Market Mood | Trade |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Jan 10 | Scared | Sold BTC |
| Jan 11 | Greedy | Bought ETH |

Now each trade is connected to the market mood.

### Step 3: Create New Metrics

I added some metrics, such as:

- Whether the trade was a winner or loser
- The size of the trade
- The return on investment
- The day of the week
- The time of day
- The level of borrowing

This helps us find patterns.

---

# What I Found Out

## Finding 1: Follow the Market Trend

The thing I found out:

### When the Market is Scared

Selling short is an idea.

People who bet on the price going down made money.

### When the Market is Greedy

Buying long is an idea.

People who bet on the price going up made money.

### Simple Rule

When the market is scared: sell short.

When the market is greedy: buy long.

This works most of the time.

---

## Finding 2: Neutral Markets Are Risky

Most traders think neutral markets are safe.

The data shows the opposite.

When the market is neutral:

- Profits are low
- Fewer trades win
- The risk of losing everything is high

This is because nobody knows what the market will do.

There is much uncertainty.

---

## Finding 3: Extreme Greed Means High Profits

When the market is really greedy:

- Profits are high
- More trades win

Traders also borrow a lot of money.

On average they borrow 15 times their investment.

This means there is a potential for profit.

One bad move can wipe out their account.

---

## Finding 4: Good Traders Are Different

The best traders do things differently.

### Good Traders

When the market is scared:

- They borrow money
- They are more careful

When the market is greedy:

- They borrow money but carefully

They adapt to the market.

### Bad Traders

- They borrow much money all the time
- They ignore the market mood
- They take risks

This results in more losses.

---

## Finding 5: The Most Common Mistake

Many traders still buy long when the market is scared.

For example:

The market is crashing.

Of following the trend they try to catch the bottom.

This results in losses.

The data shows that buying long when the market is scared is an idea.

---

## Finding 6: Borrowing Matters

### Borrowing a 1-2 times)

This gives the consistent results.

It works in every market mood.

### Borrowing a Lot (16-25 times)

This only works well when the market is strongly bullish.

Otherwise it can destroy profits.

This is a mistake in crypto trading:

> "Turn a small mistake into a big disaster quickly."

---

# Statistical Testing

I checked if the market mood directly affects profit.

Surprisingly:

The direct connection was zero.

This means:

The market mood does not directly predict profit.

It affects:

- The direction of the trade (buy or sell)
- How much money is borrowed
- How risk is managed

These decisions then affect profits.

---

# Final

The markets emotional state is useful. Not because it magically predicts profit.

Successful traders use the market mood to:

1. Choose the direction
2. Decide how much to borrow
3. Manage risk

### Best Strategy

| Market Mood | What to Do |
|-------------|------------|
| Really Scared | Sell short |
| Scared | Sell |
| Neutral | Trade less |
| Greedy | Buy long |
| Really Greedy | Buy long but be careful |
